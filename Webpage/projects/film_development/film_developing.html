Film Developing

Developing Film at home is a lot easier than you might think!

Tools and equipment

To develop Film at home, relatively little equipment has to be used.
When i was developiing my first roll of film, i used an Ilford developing kit , which came with the following:

    Equipment

  • Paterson developing Tank

  • Thermometer

  • 3x Measuring graduates

  • Film roll opener

  • 2x Film reels

  • Stirrer

  • 2x Film clips

    • Chemicals

  • Developer

  • Stop bath

  • Fixer

  • Wetting agent


    • In addition to theese tools and chemicals i used:


  • Gloves

  • Blanket

  • Distilled water

    Process

    The film i was developing was Ilford HP5 Plus.
    Ilford provides excellent data for developing times, which chemicals to use and at what temperature.
    Here is the data sheets i used for developing:

    data sheet 1
    data sheet 2
    data sheet 3

    So with this information in mind, i now had the following development data:

  • All chemicals at 20Â° C

  • For 1 spool of film, i used about 300ml of fluids in the deelopment tank

  • 6.5 minutes in ilfosol 3, concentration of 1+9

  • 10 seconds in Ilfostop, concentration 1+19

  • 2-5 minutes in Ilofrd Fixer, concentration 1+4

  • After Fixing, Rinse the film by filling the tank, invert it 10 times, drain and refill, then invert 20 times.

  • for the final rinse, use Ilfotol wetting agent, concentration 1+200

After the final rinse with wetting agent, hang the film up and leave it to dry.
When the film is dried, hope for the best that no water spots are left on the film, then you can cut the film into tinier strips, so it is more manageable :)
